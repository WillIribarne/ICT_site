Using Character.AI and Fakebook to create a fictional character's social page!

(80-min lesson plan)

Plan overview:

Students will be doing 2 main tasks: firstly, they will use Character.AI to obtain information about a character of their liking they find in the site. As they chat with it, asking the correct and desired questions, they will know more about the fictional character. After finishing this first part, they will have to open FakeBook (classtools.net) and create a web page of the character they chose. They will have to add a picture/s, bio, and some posts on their profile, relating to the information they'd gotten previously.

Requirements:

  • 1 computer per 2 students (max.)
  • Students should have an email (personal or institutional) for accessing the tools and handing in the assignment.
  • Language proficiency: A2 to B2 (complexity can be boosted according to students' lang proficiency). AI can adapt to this requirement.

Lesson start!

Task Description
(5-min) Activation: Review previous vocabulary with students that is pertinent to what they will be doing, in 1st task. Is it about hobbies? Food? Opinions about specific topics? Whatever it is, they should be ready to use it later on.
(30-min) Task 1: In groups of 2 ss, each group must go to a computer. After everyone is seated, T may show how to access the Character.AI.com website, the login process, and show an example on how to establish a conversation with a character. T can (and most likely should) write down in the whiteboard some examples of prompts as a guideline for students. They should be able to do a minimum of 4, but the more the merrier. They will later use the information they get for the next task, so tell ss not to close the tab!
(30-min) Task 2: Using the character/personality they chose, Ss will have to create a fake facebook account for them, using Fakebook (classtools.net). This will include: bio pic, profile pic, personal information, such as birthday, education, job, etc, and some posts of their liking, with a minimum of 4. T can also encourage them to use a specific grammar point (let it be tenses, phrases, expressions/idioms, etc) as long as it doesn't hinder the authenticity of the activity.
(15-min) Handing in: Ss should hand in both a transcript of the conversation they had with their character, as well as the Fakebook profile created, and sent to the teacher's online domain (of their liking, gmail, classroom, etc). Although 15 min might seem a lot of time for this process, the time-length provided should be enough for dealing with the potential conflicts that may arise when ss upload their activities to the cloud.
